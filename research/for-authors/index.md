## Writing Guidelines

### Abstract
- Maximum 300 words
- State the problem, approach, findings, and significance
- Avoid jargon and acronyms
- Make it self-contained

### Introduction
- Establish context and importance
- State the research question or objective
- Preview the approach and contributions

### Methods
- Describe approach in reproducible detail
- Justify methodological choices
- Discuss limitations

### Results
- Present findings clearly
- Use figures and tables effectively
- Distinguish results from interpretation

### Discussion
- Interpret findings in context
- Compare to related work
- Acknowledge limitations
- Suggest future directions

---

## Figure Guidelines

- High resolution (300 DPI minimum)
- Clear labels and legends
- Accessible color choices
- Self-contained captions

---

## Reference Style

Use consistent citation format. We accept:
- APA Style
- IEEE Style
- Chicago Style

Include DOIs when available.

---

## Ethics Statement

If your work involves:
- Human subjects
- Animal studies
- Sensitive data
- Potential dual-use concerns

Include an ethics statement describing:
- Approvals obtained
- Consent procedures
- Data protection measures
- Risk mitigation

---

## Data and Code

We encourage:
- Open data when possible
- Code availability
- Reproducibility artifacts

Include availability statements in your submission.

---

## Responding to Reviews

When you receive feedback:

1. **Read carefully**: Understand all comments
2. **Address systematically**: Respond point-by-point
3. **Be specific**: Describe exact changes made
4. **Be respectful**: Engage constructively
5. **Highlight changes**: Mark modifications in revised manuscript

- Missing references

### Metadata Problems
- Incomplete author info

- Incorrect paper type
